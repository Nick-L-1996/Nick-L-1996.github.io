I took ECE3829 in the Fall of 2017, which taught designing digital circuits with an FPGA. The first lab was a tutorial of the Vivado Software for programming the FPGA in Verilog. The other three lab projects had us implement a variety of circuit designs and IO.

Lab 2 had my partner and I design a circuit to drive a VGA display and respond to a light sensor. We designed the controller to drive the colors of the VGA display. The VGA display would display 1 of 4 patterns based on the input from 2 switches and vary the colors in some based on the light sensor readings. The full details can be found in the report available for download

The third lab expanded on lab 2 by adding modules that would drive a Digital to Analog Converter that would act as a function generator. This function generator could create a sinewave, a square wave, a sawtooth wave, and a triangle wave. The VGA module was modified to allow for a block on screen to be moved in a 2-dimensional grid. This lab also had us implement a test fixture in Vivado to simulate the Waveform generator. Full details can be found in the report available for download

The fourth lab added an embedded microprocessor to the FPGA called the Microblaze microcontroller. This microcontroller was programmed in C and meant to process strings input through a serial connection from a computer and output a response to a serial window. Additional circuits were reused from previous labs to drive a seven segment display for use with the microcontroller. The full details can be found in the report available for download
